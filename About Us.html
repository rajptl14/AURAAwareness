About AURA

Started with one missed signal.

AURA began as a handful of neighbors who noticed the same thing twice: a friend who caught something early, and a friend who didn't get the chance to. We built an organization around the difference between those two stories.

Our Story

Small enough to know names. Focused enough to move fast.

AURA isn't a hospital system or a national charity — we're a community fund. That means every decision, from which screening events to sponsor to which family needs a ride to an appointment, gets made by people who live here.

We route every dollar we raise to the American Cancer Society, because we believe the research and infrastructure already exist — what's missing, locally, is the push that gets people through the door.

"We didn't want to build another organization people had to explain. We wanted one people already understood the moment they heard the story."
What We Believe

Three ideas guide every dollar we move.

01

Listen early

Most people already sense when something's off. We fund the systems that take that instinct seriously, sooner.

02

Remove friction

Cost, transportation, and time off work stop more screenings than fear does. We treat logistics as part of the medicine.

03

Fund what works

We back established, evidence-backed programs rather than building new infrastructure from scratch.

How We Work

Run by volunteers, checked by a board.

01

Community board

A five-person volunteer board reviews spending each quarter and publishes a summary to donors.

02

Local partners

We work with clinics, schools, and employers already trusted in the community, rather than starting cold.

03

No paid staff

Every organizer volunteers their time, so donation dollars go to screenings and education, not salaries.

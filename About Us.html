About AURA

Started with one missed signal.

AURA began with a shared belief that early detection can change the course of a life. For Co-founder Aydin Syed, that belief came from his mother and aunt, both of whom survived cancer because their conditions were caught early. For Co-founder Raj Patel, it came through his research on Triple Negative Breast Cancer, one of the most aggressive forms of cancer, where he witnessed firsthand how early intervention can make a critical difference. Together, they built AURA to raise awareness, promote early detection, and help more people recognize health risks before it is too late.

Our Story

Small enough to know names. Focused enough to move fast.

AURA isn't a hospital system or a national charity — we're a community fund. That means every decision, from which screening events to sponsor to which family needs a ride to an appointment, gets made by people who live here.

We route every dollar we raise to the American Cancer Society, because we believe the research and infrastructure already exist — what's missing, locally, is the push that gets people through the door.

"We didn't want to build another organization people had to explain. We wanted one people already understood the moment they heard the story."
What We Believe

Three ideas guide every dollar we move.

01

Listen early

Most people already sense when something's off. We fund the systems that take that instinct seriously, sooner.

02

Remove friction

Cost, transportation, and time off work stop more screenings than fear does. We treat logistics as part of the medicine.

03

Fund what works

We back established, evidence-backed programs rather than building new infrastructure from scratch.

The Core Team

The people keeping the pulse alive.

Students from IMSA, UW-Madison, Case Western, and Georgia Tech, united by one mission.

Raj Patel
Co-Founder · UW-Madison

An incoming freshman at UW-Madison and IMSA alumnus, Raj aims to become a physician at the intersection of neuroscience and computer science. Research at Northwestern and UIC gave him hands-on experience in the lab and a deep understanding of how disease impacts real people — which drove him to co-found AURA.

Aydin Syed
Co-Founder · Case Western Reserve

Incoming freshman at Case Western and IMSA alumnus. Aydin's mother and aunt both survived breast cancer thanks to early detection — the personal experience that sparked AURA. He previously raised $1,500 for pediatric cancer research as a kid, and he's not done yet.

Krish Sav
Vice President · IMSA

Rising senior at IMSA pursuing neurosurgery and a PhD. Krish co-developed a project achieving 98.2% accuracy in diagnosing brain tumors from MRI scans. His research in oncology and neurology fuels his commitment to equitable access to early detection for underserved communities.

Maximiliano Ballesteros
Art Head · IMSA

The artist behind AURA's art prints. A senior at Illinois Math and Science Academy, Maximiliano has been drawing ever since he lost his ability to eat, which motivated him to help raise money for cancer patients. Because of people's kindness, he sees this as his way of returning that favor.

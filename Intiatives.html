Initiative 01
Student Impact

Art That Removes Barriers to Early Detection

Art for Access is a student-led art fundraiser where young artists create and donate original artwork that supporters can purchase to help improve access to lifesaving screenings. Proceeds support early detection efforts, and in the future, we hope to expand the initiative through an art gala bringing together artists, donors, and healthcare advocates.

Initiative 02

Community Education Workshops

Free evening sessions, run with local clinics, that cover what symptoms actually warrant a visit — and unlearn the idea that screening is only for people who are already worried.

Workshop A

Know your baseline

What's normal for your body, and what change is actually worth mentioning to a doctor.

Workshop B

Navigating insurance

A plain-language walkthrough of what screenings are typically covered, and how to ask.

Workshop C

Talking to family

How to raise screening with a parent or partner who's avoiding the conversation.

Initiative 03

Survivor Support Network

01

Peer matching

New patients are matched, if they want, with a local survivor who's been through a similar diagnosis.

02

Monthly meetups

An open, no-pressure gathering for patients, survivors, and caregivers to talk with people who understand.

03

Practical help fund

Small grants for the unglamorous costs of treatment — parking, childcare, meals during chemo weeks.
